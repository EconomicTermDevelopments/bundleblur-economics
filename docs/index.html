Bundleblur

Field: Behavioral Economics | JEL Codes: D11, D18, L11, M31

Definition

Intentional obfuscation through complex bundling preventing price comparison.

Etymology

Compound of "bundle" and "blur," indicating how bundling can blur price transparency and comparability.

Usage Example

Telecommunications providers frequently employ bundleblur by combining internet, phone, and streaming services at complex price points, making it nearly impossible for consumers to determine whether individual components offer competitive value compared to standalone alternatives.
